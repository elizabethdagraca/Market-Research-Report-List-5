<p><ul><li>Independent Auto Suspension System</li><li>Non-independent Auto Suspension System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The auto suspension system market is primarily divided into two types: independent and non-independent suspension systems. Independent auto suspension systems allow each wheel to move independently, enhancing ride comfort and handling, commonly used in modern vehicles. In contrast, non-independent suspension systems connect wheels on the same axle, affecting ride quality but often providing simplicity and cost-effectiveness, typically seen in older models and commercial vehicles. Both types cater to different performance, comfort, and cost requirements in the automotive industry.</p></p>
