<p><ul><li>Disproportionation Method</li><li>Hydrogenation Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Neopentyl Glycol (NPG) is produced mainly through two methods: the disproportionation and hydrogenation processes. The disproportionation method involves converting isobutylene to NPG, producing by-products like methanol, which influences downstream applications. Conversely, the hydrogenation method entails the catalytic hydrogenation of dimethyl carbonate, resulting in a purer NPG with fewer by-products. Both methods cater to diverse market demands, particularly in sectors such as plastics, paints, and coatings, with different cost structures and purity levels influencing their commercial viability.</p></p>
