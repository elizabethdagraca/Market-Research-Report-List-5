<p><p>Sultanas, or raisins, are dried grapes available in two primary market types: natural dry and artificial dry. Natural dry sultanas are produced by sun-drying grapes, preserving their natural flavor and color, resulting in a more authentic taste. In contrast, artificial dry sultanas are dehydrated using industrial methods, often involving heat or chemical processes to expedite drying. This leads to a quicker production but may alter taste and texture. Both varieties cater to different consumer preferences and applications in culinary uses.</p></p>
