<p><p>Rigging systems in markets can be categorized into manual and automatic types. Manual rigging systems rely on human intervention for tasks like price adjustments and order placements, allowing for flexibility but increasing the likelihood of errors. In contrast, automatic rigging systems use algorithms and software to manage trades and pricing in real-time, enhancing efficiency and reducing human error. Each type has its advantages: manual systems offer hands-on control, while automatic systems provide speed and precision in fast-paced market environments.</p></p>
