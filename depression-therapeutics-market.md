<p><p>The depression therapeutics market includes various classes of medications. Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs) are commonly prescribed, enhancing serotonin levels in the brain to improve mood. Selective Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors (SNRIs) target both serotonin and norepinephrine, offering alternative mechanisms for treating depression. Additionally, the "Others" category encompasses atypical antidepressants, tricyclic antidepressants, and monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs), providing diverse options for patients with different needs and responses to treatment. Together, these classes provide comprehensive approaches to managing depressive disorders.</p></p>
