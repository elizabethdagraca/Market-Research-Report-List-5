<p><p>The molecular diagnosis market for esophageal cancer features various techniques, including PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction), FISH (Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization), DNA sequencing, and gene chips. PCR amplifies specific DNA sequences to detect cancer-related mutations. FISH uses fluorescent probes to identify genetic abnormalities in tumor cells. DNA sequencing provides detailed information on the genetic composition, helping to pinpoint mutations. Gene chips analyze numerous genes simultaneously, allowing for comprehensive profiling. Together, these methods enhance early diagnosis and personalized treatment strategies for esophageal cancer.</p></p>
