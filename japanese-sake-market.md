<p><p>The Japanese sake market features several types based on brewing methods and rice polishing levels. Ordinary sake is primarily mass-produced and often less refined. Junmai is pure rice sake, with no added alcohol, offering rich flavors. Honjozo includes a small amount of distilled alcohol, enhancing fragrance. Junmai Ginjo is made from highly polished rice and is brewed with care, resulting in a delicate taste. Ginjo also has added alcohol, known for its aromatic and fruity nuances, catering to diverse palates.</p></p>
