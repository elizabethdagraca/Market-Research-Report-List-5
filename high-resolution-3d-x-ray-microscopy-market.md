<p><p>High-resolution 3D X-ray microscopy (XRM) encompasses two key market types: sub-micron XRM and nanoscale XRM. Sub-micron XRM targets imaging resolutions below one micron, making it suitable for detailed analysis of materials, biological samples, and microstructures. Nanoscale XRM, on the other hand, achieves imaging resolutions at the nanometer scale, allowing for the visualization of fine structures and nanoscale phenomena. Both technologies are crucial for advancing research in materials science, biology, and nanotechnology, enabling insights into complex 3D architectures.</p></p>
