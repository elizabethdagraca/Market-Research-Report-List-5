<p><ul><li>Contact Type</li><li>Non-Contact Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Digital wound measurement devices are categorized into two main types: contact and non-contact. Contact type devices physically touch the wound to measure dimensions and assess healing, often providing high accuracy. These include scales and calipers. Non-contact types utilize imaging technology, such as lasers or infrared sensors, to measure wounds without direct contact, reducing the risk of infection and discomfort for patients. Both types aim to enhance wound assessment, facilitate treatment planning, and improve patient outcomes in healthcare settings.</p></p>
