<p><p>The blood sample container market can be primarily categorized into two types: plastic and glass. Plastic containers are lightweight, shatter-resistant, and often designed for single-use, making them convenient for transportation and handling. They are usually made from materials like polypropylene or PET. In contrast, glass containers offer a sterile option, are inert, and provide excellent visibility of the sample, though they are heavier and more prone to breakage. Both types serve critical roles in medical and laboratory settings for safe blood storage and transport.</p></p>
