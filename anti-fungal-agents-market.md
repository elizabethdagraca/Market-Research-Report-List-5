<p><p>The anti-fungal agents market consists of various drug classes, including Azoles, Polyenes, and 5-fluorocytosine. Azoles, such as fluconazole, inhibit fungal cell membrane synthesis, making them widely used for candidiasis and other infections. Polyenes, like Amphotericin B, disrupt cell membranes, effective against severe fungal infections. 5-fluorocytosine functions by interfering with fungal RNA and protein synthesis, often used in combination therapies. Each type plays a crucial role in addressing diverse fungal infections in clinical settings.</p></p>
