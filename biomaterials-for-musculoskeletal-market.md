<p><p>The musculoskeletal biomaterials market comprises three main types: metallic, polymeric, and inorganic non-metallic biomaterials. </p><p>Metallic biomaterials, including titanium and stainless steel, are known for their strength and durability, making them suitable for orthopedic implants and fixation devices. </p><p>Polymeric biomaterials, such as polyethylene and polyurethane, offer flexibility and biocompatibility, ideal for use in prosthetics and tissue scaffolding. </p><p>Inorganic non-metallic biomaterials, like ceramics and bioactive glass, provide bioactivity and mechanical support, often used in bone repair and regeneration applications.</p></p>
