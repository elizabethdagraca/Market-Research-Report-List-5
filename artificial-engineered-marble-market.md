<p><p>The artificial engineered marble market primarily includes two types: solid surface and engineered quartz stone. Solid surface involves a non-porous material made from acrylic and polyester, offering versatility in design and ease of maintenance. Engineered quartz stone, on the other hand, consists of natural quartz crystals combined with resins, providing durability and resistance to scratches and stains. Both types cater to various applications in countertops, flooring, and wall installations, appealing to consumers seeking aesthetics, functionality, and sustainability.</p></p>
